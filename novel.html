A Possible Novel

Ethan Fast

Chapter 1

It wasn't that such thunderstorms did not have a kind of wild beauty. They did, and Bill appreciated -- had appreciated -- that, in his own way. No, it wasn't the storm he particularly cared about, one way or the other. The properties and aesthetics of thunderstorms were perfectly appropriate when put under the consideration of cool logic. Enjoy the sights. Don't go wandering on the high ground. Simple rules, of a rational nature. Clauses and stipulations that might be followed, or not, given ones particular circumstances. No, he worried over more indirect effects of the storm, certain unhealthy correlations. The sort of correlations that people tend not to talk about, the kind that sit patiently in the back of the mind.

That's exactly what they do, Bill thought. They sit patiently and cooly, like old and punctual gentlemen. Elderly little men with sparkling eyes and spiderweb wrinkles; the ones that look so gentle and kind, but that you somehow feel are quite probably not. They sit in the back the consciousness, waiting with downcast eyes and smiling, empty faces. Waiting until the onset of dusk.

It was dusk now, as Bill stared out into the light rain and darkly shining sky. It was a dusk made darker by the storm.
